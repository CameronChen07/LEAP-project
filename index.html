Learning Embodied Aesthetics for Autonomous Robotic Photography

Abstract

Photography is a compelling test bed for embodied artificial intelligence because it requires subjective aesthetic judgment tightly coupled with precise physical action in dynamic real-world scenes. We present Learning Embodied Aesthetics for Photography (LEAP), a fully autonomous robotic photography paradigm that learns human-centered composition directly from real-world demonstrations. Rather than relying on explicit heuristic rules, LEAP combines appearance-invariant compositional representations with conditional flow matching to model the multimodal trajectory distributions of expert photographers, effectively translating tacit human aesthetic preferences into an active sensorimotor policy. It is deployed through a fast-slow hierarchical controller that explicitly decouples generative aesthetic decision-making (1.2 Hz) from robust physical navigation and collision avoidance (30 Hz). Trained on only ~1.7 hours of expert motion-capture data, LEAP generalizes seamlessly to unseen human poses, indoor scenes, and unstructured outdoor environments. In physical deployments, the fully scaled system achieved a 0% failure rate across 30 complex spatial tasks while continuously adapting to dynamic subject motion. Furthermore, capturing the inherent subjectivity of photography, LEAP demonstrated significant creative variance, generating an average of two distinct, well-composed stylistic trajectories per three trials from identical initial conditions. We further show that both robustness and stylistic diversity scale monotonically with demonstration volume and heterogeneous expert data. Across objective aesthetic metrics and online user studies, LEAP significantly outperformed existing autonomous baselines, approaching human-level photographic quality. These results demonstrate that robots can reliably acquire and execute tacit, subjective aesthetic skills through continuous physical interaction, opening a path toward autonomous systems as creative partners.
