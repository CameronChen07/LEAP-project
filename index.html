Learning Embodied Aesthetics for Autonomous Robotic Photography

LEAP learns human-centered composition from expert demonstrations and turns tacit aesthetic skill into an autonomous sensorimotor policy.

Paper PDF Teaser Figure

At a glance

  • Problem: autonomous robot photography in dynamic, human-centric scenes.
  • Core idea: appearance-invariant compositional representations + conditional flow matching.
  • Control: fast-slow hierarchy, 1.2 Hz aesthetic planning, 30 Hz navigation and collision avoidance.
  • Data: only ~1.7 hours of expert motion-capture demonstrations.
  • Outcome: 0% failure in 30 complex spatial tasks, with strong aesthetic quality and stylistic diversity.

Why it matters

LEAP shows that a robot can learn a subjective creative skill, not just a fixed control objective. The method bridges perception, compositional reasoning, and real-world navigation into one deployable photography system.
